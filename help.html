Help & Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using Petition Center.

How do I start a petition?

+

Starting a petition is easy! Simply click on the "Create Campaign" link in the navigation menu, or the "Start a Petition" button on the home page. Fill out the form with your petition title, description, and target number of signatures. Then click "Create Petition" to publish it.

How many signatures should I aim for?

+

The number of signatures you should aim for depends on the scope and impact of your petition. For local issues, 100-500 signatures might be sufficient. For broader issues or those targeting major organizations, aim for 1,000 or more signatures. Set a realistic target that you believe will demonstrate significant support for your cause.

How do I sign a petition?

+

To sign a petition, browse the Active Campaigns page and find a petition you'd like to support. Click on "View Campaign" to see the full details, then click the "Sign This Petition" button. Your signature will be recorded anonymously, and the petition's signature count will increase.

What happens when a petition reaches its goal?

+

When a petition reaches its signature goal, it's automatically moved from the Active Campaigns section to the Past Campaigns section. It's marked as completed and can no longer receive signatures. The petition remains on the platform as a record of successful community action.

How can I share a petition?

+

When viewing a petition, click the "Share" button to open sharing options. You can share the petition directly to social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter, send it via email, or copy the direct link to share it anywhere else. Sharing is a powerful way to help petitions reach their signature goals faster!

Can I edit my petition after publishing it?

+

Currently, petitions cannot be edited after they're published. This ensures transparency and prevents changing the petition's purpose after people have signed it. Please review your petition carefully before publishing it.

Are my signatures anonymous?

+

Yes, signatures are anonymous by default. We track signatures using a unique identifier to prevent duplicate signing, but this information isn't publicly visible. Signatures are displayed as "Anonymous supporter" with the date.

Can't find what you're looking for?

Send us a message and we'll get back to you as soon as possible.
